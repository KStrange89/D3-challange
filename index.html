Correlations Discovered

By using the axis options given, you can look at a comparison between any two of the options. Some, like obesity v smokes, have a strong correlation. While others, like age v income, have a weaker correlation.

This is not all together surprising as people of all ages live in poverty and studies show that of the many health risks caused by smoking, obesity is one. While correlation does not imply causation, in this case, there is sufficient evidence from scientically sound studies that shows that smoking causes obesity. However, one must not look past the converse statement. Are obese people more likely to begin smoking? According to ScienceDaily, the answer is yes. The biconditionality of these studies indicates an extremely strong correlation between smoking and obesity. The reason this correlation is not even stronger can be traced to genetics and life choices.

These are just two examples of the information provided by these graphs. Feel free to view all of the graphs and conduct further research of your own to better understand the correlations among these life factors.
